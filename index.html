Spring 2026 Recap

BoatBites Had a Big Spring in Southwest Florida

From Spring Break 2026 to the Fort Myers Beach Shrimp Festival to those nightly Gulf Coast sunsets that stop everyone in their tracks, FMB showed exactly why Southwest Florida is one of the best boating markets in the country.

The energy was everywhere. Boats packed the waterways. Families, college students, locals, visitors, and weekend captains were out enjoying the water, the restaurants, the music, and the beach-town atmosphere that Fort Myers Beach is known for.

Spring Break brought the crowds. The Shrimp Festival brought the tradition. The sunsets brought everyone together at the end of the day. And through it all, one thing was clear: boaters want to stay on the water longer.

Nobody wants to pull anchor in the middle of a perfect afternoon just to go find food, grab ice, or restock drinks. Nobody wants to leave the sandbar, fight dock traffic, or interrupt the best part of the day.

That is exactly the problem BoatBites is built to solve.

BoatBites makes it simple for boaters to order cold drinks, food, ice, and essentials right to their boat. Whether you are anchored near Fort Myers Beach, tied up with friends, watching the sunset, or spending the day at your favorite local boating hangout, BoatBites helps keep the day going.

 
St. Petersburg and Tampa

BoatBites Is Back Where It All Began: St. Petersburg and Tampa

We are bringing that momentum north into two of Florida’s best boating markets: St. Petersburg and Tampa.

The Tampa Bay area is loaded with the kind of boating hangouts BoatBites was built for. Think Shell Key, Bunces Pass, Egmont Key, Passage Key, Beer Can Island, and all the local anchor-up spots where boaters spend full days on the water.

These are not quick stops. These are all-day destinations.

BoatBites is heading into our second summer, and this one feels special. After a strong spring in Southwest Florida, we are coming back to the St. Petersburg and Tampa area, back where the BoatBites idea first started taking shape.

This is where we saw the opportunity clearly: boaters spending full days on the water, anchored up with friends, enjoying the sun, the sandbars, the music, and the views, but still needing food, cold drinks, ice, and essentials without wanting to leave the water.

Boaters will be able to order food, cold drinks, ice, and essentials right to their boat, without pulling anchor, heading back to the dock, or breaking up the day. Whether you are hanging at a sandbar, tied up with friends, cruising near the islands, or watching the sunset from the water, BoatBites helps keep the day going.

Second summer. Bigger market. More ways to serve boaters.

From Bunces Pass to John’s Pass, BoatBites is back where it all began.
